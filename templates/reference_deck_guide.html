Game Plan
T1–T2: Deploy + Burn
Mountain → Goblin Guide (2 damage immediately) or suspend Rift Bolt. T2: Monastery Swiftspear + burn spell = prowess trigger for 4+ damage. Eidolon on T2 punishes opponent for casting spells. Target: 6-8 damage by end of T2.
T3–T4: Reach Phase
Price of Progress hits for 4-8 damage against dual land manabases (most of Legacy). Chain Lightning and Lava Spike for face damage. Prowess triggers on Swiftspear. Target: opponent at 4-6 life.
T4–T5: Fireblast Finish
Fireblast (sacrifice 2 Mountains, 4 damage for free) is the closer. Can double-Fireblast for 8 damage if you have 4 Mountains. Average kill: T4.2 — faster than any fair deck in Legacy.

Danger Matchups — How to Steal Games
Burn is the format's speed police. It forces every deck to win by T4 or have lifegain. Decks that spend early turns setting up (control, tempo, ramp) get punished — 82-100% WR. The only decks that threaten Burn are faster combo (Depths 42%, Oops 44%, Reanimator 46%) that can kill before Burn assembles 20 damage.
By Archetype
vs Control/Ramp (88-100%): Free wins. They tap out for card advantage while you kill them.
vs Tempo (82%): Their Force of Will costs 1 life + 1 card. You have 28 burn spells — they can't counter all of them.
vs Aggro (74%): Eidolon punishes their cheap spells. Price of Progress punishes their manabase. You're faster.
vs Combo (65%): The danger zone. Fast combo (Depths, Reanimator, Oops) can goldfish before you deal 20. Slow combo (Doomsday, Storm) still loses because their setup takes too long.

Why Burn is #1
Smallest flat→weighted drop (-5.0pp) of any T1 deck.
